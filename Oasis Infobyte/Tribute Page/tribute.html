Bharat Ratna Lata Mangeshkar (1929-2022)

Lata Mangeshkar, born on September 28, 1929, in Indore, India, is hailed as one of the greatest playback singers in the history of Indian cinema. With a career spanning over seven decades, Lata Ji, as she is affectionately known, has mesmerized audiences with her soulful voice, impeccable artistry, and unparalleled versatility. This tribute website celebrates the life, legacy, and extraordinary contributions of the Nightingale of India.

Born : 28 September 1929, Indore, Indore State, British India (present-day Madhya Pradesh, India)
Died : 6 February 2022 (aged 92) Mumbai, Maharashtra, India
Honours :
  • Padma Bhushan (1969)
  • Dadasaheb Phalke Award (1989)
  • Maharashtra Bhushan (1997)
  • Padma Vibhushan (1999)
  • Bharat Ratna (2001)
  • Officer of the National Order of the Legion of Honour (2006)
    • Early Life : Lata Mangeshkar was born into a family deeply rooted in musical tradition. Her father, Pandit Deenanath Mangeshkar, was a classical singer and theater actor, while her mother, Shevanti (later renamed Shudhamati), was also musically inclined. Lata was the eldest among five siblings, including sisters Asha, Usha, and brothers Hridaynath and Meena. Growing up in such a musically immersive environment, Lata's passion for music was nurtured from an early age. At the tender age of 13, Lata had to shoulder the responsibility of supporting her family financially. She began her professional career as a playback singer in Hindi and Marathi films, taking on various singing assignments to contribute to the family's income.
    Singing Career : Lata Mangeshkar is one of the most iconic and celebrated playback singers in the history of Indian cinema. Her singing career spans over seven decades, during which she has recorded songs for thousands of Bollywood movies as well as in various regional languages. Her breakthrough came with the song "Aayega Aanewala" from the movie Mahal (1949), composed by music director Khemchand Prakash. She collaborated with legendary music directors such as S.D. Burman, Shankar-Jaikishan, R.D. Burman, Naushad, and many others. Even though she officially retired from playback singing in 2013, her songs continue to be cherished by music lovers worldwide. Lata Mangeshkar's contribution to Indian music and cinema is immeasurable, and her legacy as one of the greatest playback singers of all time remains unparalleled.
